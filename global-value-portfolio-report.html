Secular Tailwinds Driving 10-Year Compounding
驱动十年复利的长期趋势
This portfolio sits at the intersection of the most powerful multi-decade trends in the global economy. These are not cyclical bets — they are structural shifts with 15-30 year runways that our holdings are positioned to capture.
1. Global Cash-to-Digital Payments Transition
$18.7T → $33.5T+ by 2030
Digital payments spending surged from $1.7T in 2014 to $18.7T in 2024, and is projected to exceed $33.5T by 2030. The digital payment solutions market is growing at 21.4% CAGR. US cash use fell from 44% of in-store spending in 2014 to just 15% in 2024. Emerging markets are still early innings — Asia-Pacific alone captured 38.7% of digital payment value. Beneficiaries: Mastercard (MA), American Express (AXP) — 20% of portfolio.
2. AI & Semiconductor Supercycle
$628B → $1.8T Semiconductor Market by 2030
SEMI forecasts the semiconductor market reaching $1.8T by 2030. AI chip demand is driving a structural shift: TSMC's CoWoS capacity doubling to 660,000 wafers in 2025, with AI accelerator revenue growing at mid-to-high 50% CAGR through 2029. TSMC's foundry market share climbed from 59% (2023) to 66% (2025) — the gap to competitors is widening. Advanced nodes below 7nm are growing at 12% annually with utilization exceeding 90%. Beneficiaries: TSMC (TSM), Alphabet (GOOGL), Apple (AAPL) — 35% of portfolio.
3. India's Structural Economic Boom
$3.9T → $7.3T GDP by 2030
India is the world's fastest-growing major economy at 7.8% GDP growth (Q1 FY2025-26). S&P upgraded India's sovereign rating to BBB for the first time in 18 years. Morgan Stanley projects India's GDP to more than double this decade to $7.5T. Credit-to-GDP ratio expected to increase from 57% to 100% — massive runway for HDFC Bank. India's digital economy projected to reach 20% of GDP by 2030. Consumption to more than double from $2T to $4.9T. Beneficiaries: HDFC Bank (HDB), Infosys (INFY) — 18% of portfolio.
4. China's AI & Consumer Internet Renaissance
HSI at 10-Year Highs Despite Discount Multiples
Chinese equities reached 10-year highs in 2025, lifted by stimulus measures and the OpenClaw AI revolution. Tencent's revenue grew 14% in 2025; Alibaba maintains wide economic moat with profitable e-commerce monetization for over a decade. Both companies are aggressively returning capital via buybacks (Tencent: HK$112B in 2024). Despite strong business performance, both trade at deep discounts to US peers — the "China discount" creates Graham-style margin of safety. Beneficiaries: Tencent (0700.HK), Alibaba (9988.HK) — 19% of portfolio.

Risk Analysis & Bear Case Scenarios
风险分析与熊市情景
No investment thesis is complete without an honest assessment of what could go wrong. Per Munger's inversion principle: "Tell me where I'm going to die, so I'll never go there." Below are the four scenarios that could cause this portfolio to materially underperform.
Scenario 1: Prolonged US-China Cold War
Probability: 25% • Portfolio Impact: -15% drawdown • Exposure: 19% (Tencent + Alibaba)
Chinese ADR delisting, capital controls, or severe trade restrictions could impair Tencent and Alibaba. However, both are primarily listed in Hong Kong, have strong domestic revenue bases, and are returning massive capital to shareholders. The "China discount" already prices in significant pessimism — Alibaba at 9x earnings has limited further downside vs. fundamentals. Our mitigation: the 19% allocation is sized as a moderate conviction bet, not a concentrated one.
Scenario 2: AI Disrupts Current Moats Faster Than Expected
Probability: 15% • Portfolio Impact: -20% on affected names • Exposure: GOOGL, INFY, MA
If AI agents replace Google Search as the primary information interface, Alphabet's ad model is at risk. If AI code generation commoditizes IT services, Infosys margins compress. If AI-powered crypto/A2A rails bypass card networks, Mastercard loses share. Counter-argument: Alphabet IS the leading AI company (Gemini, TPU, Waymo). TSMC manufactures all AI chips regardless of winner. Mastercard's network effects took 50 years to build — disruption won't happen overnight.
Scenario 3: Global Recession + Rate Shock
Probability: 20% • Portfolio Impact: -25% drawdown, recovery in 2-3 years • Exposure: Broad
High-multiple US tech names (Apple 28x, TSMC 23x, Mastercard 35x) could see 30-40% drawdowns in a severe recession. However, the defensive ballast (Coca-Cola — 62 years of dividend growth, recession-resistant), deep-value names (Alibaba at 9x), and India's counter-cyclical growth (6.5%+ GDP even in global downturns) would cushion the portfolio. Historical precedent: quality portfolios recover faster from drawdowns because earnings power is resilient.
Scenario 4: Taiwan Strait Crisis
Probability: 10% • Portfolio Impact: -30-40% drawdown on TSMC • Exposure: 12%
Low probability but catastrophic tail risk if realized. TSMC has 80%+ of advanced production in Taiwan. Mitigants: TSMC is building fabs in US (Arizona), Japan (Kumamoto), and Germany (Dresden). A Taiwan crisis would devastate the entire global tech supply chain — TSMC's stock wouldn't be uniquely affected. This is a systemic risk, not a stock-specific risk. Our mitigation: TSMC is sized at 12% (moderate conviction), not 15%.
